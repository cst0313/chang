02 Origin

I grew up where the harbour
meets the city.

Hong Kong has a horizon that bends. Sea on one side, vertical city on the other, and a strip of weather running between them that no one has ever quite agreed on.

I learned, early, that one careful measurement — taken at the right place, by someone who is paying attention — can change everything you thought you knew about the system around it.

03 Selected Instruments

A short, deliberate list. The years are accurate. The brevity is intentional.

  1. I. 2022 Hong Kong

    A neural network for the unseen.

    A deep network that removes monochromatic metal artefacts from CT scans, so radiologists see what was hidden in the noise. Awarded the S.T. Yau High School Science Award in Computer Science — a prize founded by the Fields medalist Shing-Tung Yau.

    Deep learningMedical imagingYau Prize
  2. II. 2023 Shing Mun River

    Project Oyster.

    A physics-informed neural network that learns to predict where ocean currents will carry microplastics next, by training on the Navier-Stokes equations themselves. Champion of the HKSTP–St. John's College Hackathon. Currently a working prototype for environmental monitoring agencies in Hong Kong.

    PINNsFluid dynamicsClimateHKSTP champion
  3. III. 2024 Imperial College

    A board that lives in superposition.

    A web interface, written from scratch, so anyone can play chess against a quantum neural network. The pieces occupy several squares at once until you look. Built to teach quantum intuition to people who have never written a line of Qiskit.

    Quantum MLAmazon BraketInteractive
  4. IV. 2024 Laidlaw Foundation

    Auditing the price of weather.

    A research report — Are climate finance models fit for purpose? — examining whether the models steering billions of dollars in climate spend are accurate enough to deserve the steering wheel. Selected for the Laidlaw research and leadership programme as a member of the 2024 cohort.

    ResearchFinancePolicy
  5. V. 2025 Canary Wharf

    Instruments for markets that do not sleep.

    Summer at Qube Research & Technologies, a quantitative investment manager that runs on research the way a lighthouse runs on electricity. Working alongside people who treat a tenth of a basis point the way a watchmaker treats a tenth of a second.

    Quantitative researchSystematic trading
